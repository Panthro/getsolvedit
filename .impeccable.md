# Impeccable — design context (getsolvedit)

This file captures visual and UX direction for AI-assisted work in this repo. Update the **Design Context** section when positioning or aesthetics change.

## Design Context

### Users

Small business owners (hospitality, consumer services, recreation, beauty/food retail, and adjacent trades) who arrive from **cold email** or shared links with campaign query params. They are often on **mobile**, short on time, and mildly skeptical of software pitches. The job to be done: **understand the offer in seconds**, see that the pain is real for their trade, and **join a waitlist** with minimal friction—without feeling tricked or “enterprise sold.”

### Brand Personality

Aligned with the **Sara Morgan** outbound persona: **friendly, direct, human**—a solo founder testing demand, not a sales team. Copy elsewhere should stay **non-salesy** and honest about “something I’m building.” Visually: **approachable and legible**, with enough specificity in custom landings that each vertical feels seen. Emotional target: **calm confidence and clarity** rather than hype, urgency, or intimidation.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Typography:** [Outfit](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Outfit) (weights 600–800 in the root layout) exposed as `font-brand`; body text often uses the default sans stack via Tailwind.

**Signature mark:** The **get** / **solved** / **it** wordmark uses **sky, violet, and amber** (`BrandLogo` tones). Treat these as the anchor palette for brand recognition.

**Surfaces:**

- **Generic template** (`GenericLanding`): light **white** canvas, **blue-600** primary actions, **gray** body copy, **blue-50** pills—clean, readable, slightly conventional SaaS clarity.
- **Custom campaign landings** (e.g. menu): **vertical-specific** palettes—warm **stone** neutrals with **amber** accents, soft gradients, light **backdrop-blur** headers, and contextual illustrations where they aid understanding—not decoration for its own sake.

**Theme:** App shell is **light mode** today; no shared dark theme in layout.

**Anti-patterns for this product stage:** Fake scarcity, crowded “startup” hero stacks, muddy contrast, or visuals that contradict the **plain-text, no-BS** email voice.

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — One dominant story: headline, support line, benefits, single primary waitlist path. Avoid ornamental UI that competes with the message.
2. **Founder-credible** — Look and copy should reinforce **trust** for a small operator, not a faceless corporation or aggressive growth funnel.
3. **Vertical truth in bespoke pages** — When the generic template is too generic, use **custom landings** with palette and layout that match the business context—without breaking **Tally** wiring or **PostHog** events.
4. **Stable conversion plumbing** — Preserve `TrackedWaitlistCta`, `LandingAnalytics`, `buildTallyHref` / embed behavior, and hidden fields (`idea`, `mkt`, `lang`, `src`) when changing layout or styles.
5. **Accessible baselines** — Semantic structure, visible focus, readable type sizes; aim for **WCAG AA** contrast on new text/background pairs unless a specific exception is documented.
